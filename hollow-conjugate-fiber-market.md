<p><p>The Hollow Conjugate Fiber market includes two primary types: Hollow Conjugated Siliconized Fiber and Hollow Conjugated Non-silicified Fiber. Siliconized fibers are treated with silicone to enhance softness, durability, and resistance to clumping, making them ideal for premium applications like pillows and comforters. Non-silicified fibers lack this treatment, offering a more cost-effective option for bulkier products like mattress fillings. Both types leverage hollow structures for lightweight, insulating properties, catering to diverse needs in the textile and bedding industries.</p></p>
